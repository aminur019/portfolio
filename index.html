Frequently Asked Questions

Answer: A web developer creates, maintains, and optimizes websites, ensuring they are functional, visually appealing, and responsive across devices. They work on either the front-end (design/UI) or back-end (server/database), or both (full-stack), using programming languages and technologies to deliver fast, secure, and user-friendly websites.

1. What does a web developer do?

Answer: A web developer builds and maintains websites. They work on the technical aspects, such as site functionality, speed, and design, ensuring the site is user-friendly and performs well on different devices.

2. What are the differences between front-end, back-end, and full-stack development?

Answer:
i.    Front-end:- Works on the user-facing part (HTML, CSS, JavaScript).
ii.   Back-end:- Focuses on server-side logic (databases, servers, APIs).
iii.  Full-stack:- Covers both front-end and back-end development.

3. What is responsive web design?

Answer: Responsive design ensures that websites adapt to different screen sizes and devices, offering an optimal user experience across desktops, tablets, and smartphones.

4. How can I make my website load faster?

Answer: You can improve loading speed by optimizing images, minifying CSS/JavaScript, using a Content Delivery Network (CDN), and enabling browser caching.

5. What is your process for starting a new project?

Answer: The process generally involves:

i.    Understanding client requirements.
ii.   Planning and wireframing.
iii.  Designing, developing, and testing.
iv.  Launching and ongoing maintenance.
